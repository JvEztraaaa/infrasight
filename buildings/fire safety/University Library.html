University Library

Building Information

Year of Construction: 2004

No. of Storey: 6-storey

Building Type: Office and School

View Infrastructure Audit

The University Library, constructed in 2004, is a 6-storey reinforced concrete frame mixed-use building serving as both an academic and administrative hub. Designed to accommodate office spaces and school functions, it houses extensive collections of print and digital resources, study areas, and research facilities.

Recommendation

Based on the findings from the inspection, it is recommended that the Library immediately improve its fire safety compliance by addressing several key deficiencies. First, a sprinkler system should be installed to enhance the building's capability to suppress fires at an early stage, especially considering the high fire load in libraries due to paper materials. While a fire hose is available, it must be enclosed in a proper cabinet to protect it from damage and ensure easy access during emergencies. Additionally, smoke detectors should be installed on all floor levels to provide early warning in the event of fire or smoke. The absence of an evacuation plan is a critical concern; a detailed and visible evacuation plan must be posted in strategic areas to guide occupants during emergencies.
