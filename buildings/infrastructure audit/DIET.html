Engineering Building (DIET)

Building Information

Year of Construction: 1998

No. of Storey: 3-storey

Building Type: Office and School

View Fire Safety Inspection

The Engineering Building (3-storey) is an office and school building used by the Department of Industrial Engineering and Technology (DIET) students and faculty. It was constructed in the year 2006, designed to serve as a learning and office space.

Accessibility Features:

Recommendation

The physical inspection revealed the presence of minor structural defects, such as diagonal cracks between walls and columns, as well as a chipped and uneven first stair step, minor ceiling holes, discolored and broken ceiling panels, minor floor slab cracks, multiple chipped slabs, and exposed wiring due to outlet breakage, all of which could pose safety and operational risks if left unaddressed. Given these observations, it is recommended that a detailed structural evaluation be conducted by qualified professionals to assess the severity of the cracks and the overall load-bearing capacity of the building. Immediate repairs should be made to reinforce damaged stairs, seal ceiling holes, and replace broken or discolored panels to restore aesthetic and functional integrity. The uneven stair step should be corrected to prevent tripping hazards, while electrical outlets with exposed wiring must be urgently fixed to avoid fire or shock incidents.
