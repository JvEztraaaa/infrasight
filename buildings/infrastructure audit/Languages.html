Languages and Humanities Building (CAS)

Building Information

Year of Construction: 2002

No. of Storey: 3-storey

Building Type: Office and School

View Fire Safety Inspection

The Languages and Humanities Building, commonly referred to as the College of Arts and Sciences (CAS), is a key academic facility that fosters interdisciplinary learning in the fields of language, humanities, and the sciences. It encompasses both the Physical Sciences and Biological Sciences Buildings, which provide essential spaces for classroom instruction, laboratory experimentation, and scholarly engagement.

Recommendation

Physically, the building exhibits several concerns, including cracks extending to doors and windows, minor tile cracks, a chipped exterior column, corroded and dusty windows, as well as severely worn-out doors and electrical outlets. The building is also classified as highly vulnerable to landslides. Given these findings, it is recommended that preventive slope stabilization measures be implemented immediately to mitigate the risk of landslides. Moreover, Targeted repairs such as sealing cracks around openings, replacing corroded windows, and repairing or replacing damaged doors and electrical fixtures should be undertaken to improve both functionality and safety. Furthermore, regular maintenance and inspection schedules should be enforced to ensure the continued performance and reliability of the building systems.
