International House 1

Building Information

Year of Construction: 2008

No. of Storey: 3-storey

Building Type: Office and Residential

View Fire Safety Inspection

The International House 1, constructed in 2008, is a mixed-use facility that serves both office and residential functions. Designed to accommodate faculty offices, research spaces, and living quarters, the building supports a dynamic environment for academic collaboration and staff residency. Its dual-purpose structure allows for close integration between professional and residential use, catering to the needs of researchers, administrators, and other personnel.

Recommendation

Notable issues found include wooden ceilings in poor condition, multiple minor cracks on walls and window areas, a minor wall-to-column separation, exposed and unsecured electrical wiring, and roofing elements in need of significant repair. Given these conditions, it is recommended that the university initiate a replacement of deteriorated wooden ceilings, sealing of cracks, and repair of the minor wall-column separations should be undertaken to prevent escalation into more serious structural issues. Additionally, the exposed electrical outlets must be properly enclosed and secured to meet fire and electrical safety standards. Roofing improvements should include inspection for leaks, rust, or loose panels and installation of proper drainage systems to ensure occupant safety and weather protection. Lastly, as the site is highly susceptible to landslides, slope stabilization measures and terrain monitoring should be implemented.
