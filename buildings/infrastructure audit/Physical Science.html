Physical Science Building

Building Information

Year of Construction: 1992

No. of Storey: 2-storey

Building Type: Office and School

View Infrastructure Audit

The Physical Science Building, established in 1992, functions as both an academic hub and administrative office for the physical sciences department. It accommodates classrooms, faculty offices, and laboratories, supporting a wide range of instructional and research activities. As one of the older structures on campus, it plays a significant role in the academic operations of the college. With its dual-purpose design for education and office use, the building's safety, accessibility, and structural integrity are vital to ensuring a secure and productive environment for students, faculty, and staff.

Accessibility Features:
- PWD Ramp with Railings

Recommendation

Based on the assessment, several safety-related deficiencies were noted, such as the unequal rise on the first step of a stair, worn-out wooden handrails, displacement of steel handrails that require urgent redesign. Aging ceiling and electrical outlets were also found which present potential hazards to building occupants. To mitigate these concerns, it is recommended that immediate repairs and corrective actions be undertaken to restore the structural and non-structural integrity of the building. This includes retrofitting staircases, replacing and redesigning the handrails for safety compliance, and conducting minor renovations on the ceiling and electrical systems. Moreover, site-level geohazard mitigation measures such as soil stabilization or slope reinforcement should be explored to reduce the risks associated with liquefaction and landslide.
