Biological Sciences Building

Building Information

Year of Construction: 1997

No. of Storey: 2-storey

Building Type: Office and School

View Fire Safety Inspection

The Biological Sciences Building, constructed in 1997, serves as both an academic and administrative facility within the campus. Designed to support the teaching and research needs of the biological sciences department, the building houses lecture rooms, faculty offices, and laboratory spaces.

- PWD Ramp

Recommendation

Notable observations include several cracks on the floor slab, bulging ceilings, peeling paint on ceilings and columns, and a missing or damaged section of the roof. Moreover, the presence of stagnant dirty water with moss and algae along the building's side poses not only a sanitation concern but also a risk for slip-related accidents and long-term degradation of the adjacent structural components. In response to these conditions, it is recommended that the Biological Sciences Building undergo an in-depth structural integrity assessment by professional engineers to evaluate the extent of internal damage and prioritize necessary retrofitting or rehabilitation work, especially on the floor slab and roofing system. Measures should also be taken to improve drainage and eliminate the buildup of stagnant water to reduce environmental and health risks.
