College of Criminal Justice (CCJ)

Building Information

Year of Construction: 2018

No. of Storey: 4-storey

Building Type: Office and School

View Fire Safety Inspection

The College of Criminal Justice, established in 2018, is a modern academic facility that functions as both an office and instructional building dedicated to the field of law enforcement and criminology. It houses faculty offices, classrooms, and specialized training areas designed to support a comprehensive curriculum for criminal justice students.

Recommendation

Several critical issues were identified during the inspection. These include hidden leaks in comfort rooms (CR) , a non-functional poso negro system that flooded upon occupancy, inadequate water supply reaching the upper floors, insufficient electrical outlets, and multiple minor cracks on structural elements such as columns and walls. In light of these findings, it is recommended that plumbing systems should be reviewed and repaired to eliminate leaks and restore the functionality of the poso negro system, ensuring sanitary safety. Water pressure solutions, such as booster pumps, should be considered to ensure reliable water supply on the 3rd and 4th floors. Electrical upgrades must also be carried out to address the shortage of outlets and prevent overload hazards. Lastly, while the structural cracks are currently minor, a detailed structural assessment should be conducted to determine the necessity for preventive reinforcement and to maintain the overall integrity and safety of the building.
