University Library

Building Information

Year of Construction: 2004

No. of Storey: 6-storey

Building Type: Office and School

View Fire Safety Inspection

The University Library, constructed in 2004, is a 6-storey reinforced concrete frame mixed-use building serving as both an academic and administrative hub. Designed to accommodate office spaces and school functions, it houses extensive collections of print and digital resources, study areas, and research facilities.

Accessibility Features:
- PWD Ramp
- Elevator with Braille system
- PWD parking

Recommendation

To improve the safety and functionality of the library, several structural and functional enhancements are necessary. On the 6th floor, repairs should address cracked room partitions and the installation of tiles across the entire floor to prevent tripping hazards. The emergency exit stairs are also in urgent need of spalling repairs and tile replacement to ensure safe evacuation routes. Additionally, the parking lot should be upgraded with proper concrete pavement to support accessibility and durability. Other essential improvements include replacing damaged electrical outlets and repairing broken windows to enhance both security and insulation. For a clearer understanding of the structural issues, photographs of the identified defects are provided for reference.
