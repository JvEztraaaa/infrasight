The College of Engineering Information and Technology, or CEIT, was constructed in 2006. A two-storey building used as an office for faculties of Department of Engineering and Architecture (DCEA), Department of Computer, Electronics, and Electrical Engineering (DCEEE), Department of Industrial Engineering and Technology (DIET), Department of Information Technology (DIT), Department of Agriculture and Food Engineering (DAFE), and Physical Planning Unit (PPU).

Recommendation

it is strongly recommended that the building undergo repair and reinforcement of the severely cracked walls found on the building to prevent structural failure during seismic events. Additionally, to address geohazard vulnerabilities, soil stabilization and the construction of retaining structures or slope protection measures should be considered. Moreover, regular maintenance and inspection schedules should be instituted to monitor the condition of non-structural components and auxiliary systems. or a clearer understanding of the structural issues, photographs of the identified defects are provided for reference.
